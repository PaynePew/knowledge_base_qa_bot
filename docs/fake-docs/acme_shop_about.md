# Acme Shop

Acme Shop is a fictional e-commerce company used as a demo fixture for the `knowledge_base_qa_bot` ingest pipeline. This file lives in `docs/fake-docs/` — the unified synthetic Source pool that serves as the runtime demo KB, the Phase 8 eval corpus source, and the user-facing folder-import example (issue #142).

## Company History

Acme Shop was founded in 2018 by two college friends, Jordan Rivera and Alex Chen, who were frustrated by the lack of affordable, high-quality kitchen gadgets online. They launched from a garage in Austin, Texas, with a catalog of 12 products and $15,000 in seed capital from friends and family.

By 2020, Acme Shop had grown to 150 SKUs and a team of 8 full-time employees. The COVID-19 pandemic accelerated demand for home cooking supplies, and revenue tripled in that year alone. In 2022, the company raised a $3 million Series A round to fund warehouse expansion and technology improvements.

Today, Acme Shop ships to all 50 US states and 12 international markets, with over 500 active products across kitchen, dining, and home-organization categories.

## Mission

Acme Shop's mission is to make thoughtful, well-designed home products accessible to everyone — not just those who can afford premium retail prices. Every product in the catalog is sourced, tested, and reviewed by the in-house product team before listing.

The company operates on three principles:

1. **Quality first**: No product is listed unless it passes the internal 30-point quality checklist.
2. **Fair pricing**: Margins are kept lean to offer competitive prices without compromising quality.
3. **Customer trust**: Returns are accepted within 30 days, no questions asked.

## Locations

Acme Shop's headquarters is in Austin, Texas. The primary fulfillment warehouse is in Columbus, Ohio, chosen for its proximity to the US population center. A second warehouse in Phoenix, Arizona, was opened in 2023 to improve delivery times to the West Coast.
